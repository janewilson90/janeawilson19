About Me

Who am I?

I'm a UX Analyst based at Waracle in Dundee. Mainly spend my time deep in Google Analytics, running A/B tests, and creating bespoke reports for clients. My day to day focus is ensuring that our products continue to meet the expectations of our clients and clients end-users. This achieved through monitoring of KPIs, A/B testing, and funnel creation to monitor user flow.

I also create detailed monthly and weekly reports for clients outlining their end-users usage of the app/website, how engaged their users have been, app store reviews and rating breakdowns and give suggestions for improvements or future developments which may be required to ensure the app/website continues to be of use to end-users.

I'm also involved with the running of workshops with new clients, helping to define the project scope and risks taking on the role of a business analyst. This involves planning the workshops to elicit requirements from clients, analysing workshop outputs, evaluating requirements based on risk, benefit and time to develop, and communicating back to the client what we feel is the best MVP of their product.

I routinely give talks at Waracle's Brownbags (lunchtime quick talks on anything) and have spoken at tech meetups. Topics have included, attention - what it is and how it works, baby duck syndrome (how users adapt to change), designing for low literacy populations, the psychology of choice, why your users are ghosting you, and how Disney uses mind control.
